In situ Monitoring Ocean Parks

Advancing Marine Conservation through Real-Time Environmental Surveillance

Nature Research is a leading scientific publisher committed to advancing sustainable ocean conservation.

We organize global conferences to bring together scientists, policymakers, and technologists.

This conference focuses on real-time monitoring of marine protected areas using in situ technologies.

Our mission is to foster innovation in ocean observation and data-driven conservation strategies.

We collaborate with academic institutions, NGOs, and government agencies worldwide.

Our editorial teams from Nature Climate Change, Nature Sustainability, and Nature Communications are involved.

Proceedings will be published in a special issue of a Nature journal.

We ensure rigorous peer review and high scientific standards.

Contact us for partnership or media opportunities.

Follow us on social media for updates.

Venue & Travel

Location: Athens, Greece

Venue: Stavros Niarchos Foundation Cultural Center (SNFCC)

A world-class facility located by the Saronic Gulf, combining sustainability, architecture, and technology.

The conference will be held in the state-of-the-art auditorium and exhibition halls.

Accommodation: Special rates available at partner hotels in central Athens.

Transport: The venue is accessible via metro, bus, and taxi. Free shuttle from major hotels.

Local Attractions: Acropolis, National Garden, Plaka, and coastal walks.

Climate: September in Athens is warm and sunny, averaging 28°C (82°F).
